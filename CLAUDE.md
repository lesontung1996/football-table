## Design Context

### Users
Casual football fans and friends running informal leagues or tournaments use this app to quickly set up games, generate random matchups, and track how their mini-league is going without needing a complex admin tool.

### Brand Personality
Playful, fun, and energetic, while still feeling reliable enough that people trust the randomization and results. Copy and interactions should feel encouraging and light, avoiding anything overly serious or corporate.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark, rich FPL-inspired theme using vibrant accent colors (like the existing FPL-style palette) against a deep background. Interfaces should feel lively and game-like, with clear hierarchy, strong typography, and UI that feels closer to a polished sports product than a generic dashboard.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize clarity under excitement – even when things animate or feel playful, results, buttons, and status should always be unambiguous at a glance.
2. Respect the users’ time – common flows (creating a league, spinning the wheel, viewing schedule/table) should be accessible in 1–2 obvious actions with minimal required input.
3. Celebrate moments – emphasize key interactions like drawing teams, confirming fixtures, or finishing rounds with subtle but satisfying motion and microcopy.
4. Stay legible in dark mode – maintain strong contrast and readable type across the dark theme, especially for numbers, team names, and outcomes.
